# My Trip to Fushimi Inari Shrine

I finally visited **Fushimi Inari Taisha** during my trip to Kyoto!
By getting there early in the morning, I was able to enjoy the iconic torii gates without the crowds.

## Basic Info

- **Address**: 68 Fukakusa Yabunouchicho, Fushimi-ku, Kyoto
- **Access**: Right outside JR Inari Station (Nara Line)
- **Admission**: Free
- **Official Website**: [Fushimi Inari Taisha](https://inari.jp/en/)

## Senbon Torii (Thousand Torii Gates)

This is what Fushimi Inari is all about. The endless rows of vermillion torii gates are absolutely breathtaking.

![Senbon Torii](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/shinya/image-storage/refs/heads/master/bokuchi/senbon-torii.jpg)

Photos don't do it justice — the real thing is far more impressive. The morning sunlight filtering through the gates created an almost magical atmosphere.

### Packing Checklist

- [x] Comfortable walking shoes
- [x] Water bottle (vending machines are rare near the top)
- [x] Towel
- [x] Camera
- [ ] Tripod (bringing one next time for sure)

## Food & Souvenirs

The approach to the shrine is lined with food stalls and shops. Here are my highlights:

| Item | Place | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Kitsune Udon | Tea house on the path | Rich dashi broth — amazing |
| Inari Sushi | Nezameya | Juicy, flavorful fried tofu wraps |
| Matcha Soft Serve | Street vendor | Perfect on a warm day |

## Final Thoughts

If you're visiting Fushimi Inari, **go early in the morning**. Fewer tourists, better photos, and a peaceful atmosphere.
The hike to the summit is rewarding both for the exercise and the stunning views of Kyoto.

I'd love to come back during autumn for the fall foliage.
